## Overview

The **Ω-Field Theory** is a pre-geometric framework in which spacetime, Standard Model structures, and dark-sector phenomena emerge from a trinitary field:

\[
\Omega(x) = \bigl(\phi(x), M_{\mu\nu}(x), A_\mu(x)\bigr).
\]

Each component represents an operational mode:

- **Substance** — scalar rhythm \( \phi \)  
- **Form** — tensor rhythm \( M_{\mu\nu} \)  
- **Relation** — vector rhythm \( A_\mu \)

The theory is governed by **five differential operators**:

1. Movement — kinetic propagation  
2. Polarity — vector asymmetry  
3. Duality — tensor–vector mixing  
4. Rhythm — conservation constraints  
5. Generation — spectral selection of stable modes  

These operators define the structural algebra responsible for the emergence of geometry and particle masses.

## Key Results

### **1. Emergent Geometry**
The metric arises as a two-point correlator of rhythmic modes:
\[
g_{\mu\nu} = \Re \langle R_\mu, R_\nu \rangle.
\]

Einstein gravity is recovered with small Higgs-suppressed corrections:
\[
G_{\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{v^2}\Box G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G_N T_{\mu\nu}.
\]
---

### **2. Yukawa Hierarchies from Operator Spectrum**
The 12 fermion masses of the Standard Model arise from the eigenvalues of the generative operator \( \mathcal{G} \):

\[
m_f = v\,\lambda_f, \qquad 
\lambda_f \in \text{Spec}(\mathcal{G}).
\]

No arbitrary Yukawa parameters are introduced; all hierarchies are spectral.

A block-diagonal structure  
\[
\mathcal{G} = \bigoplus_{k=1}^4 G_k
\]
naturally yields the 12 observed fermion masses (quarks, charged leptons, neutrinos).
---

### **3. Dark Sector and Fuzzy Dark Matter**
A sterile scalar mode \( \omega_4 \) survives symmetry breaking and acquires an exponentially suppressed mass via tunneling:

\[
m_{\omega_4} \sim v\,e^{-S}, \qquad S \approx 100.
\]

This gives:
\[
m_{\omega_4} \sim 10^{-22}\,\mathrm{eV},
\]
consistent with fuzzy dark matter phenomenology.
---

### **4. Observable Predictions**
- Area quantization: \( \Delta A \sim \ell_P^2 \)  
- MOND-like correction: \( a_0 \approx 1.2\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m/s^2} \)  
- Scalar–tensor gravitational waves with amplitude ratio \( \gamma \sim 10^{-3} \)
